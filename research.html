Research

At the Translational Organ Microsystems Group, we develop biomimetic and predictive human organ models to bridge the gap between preclinical discovery and clinical translation, with a particular focus on the lung.

Our research is driven by a central question:

Can we engineer human organ microsystems that faithfully predict disease progression and therapeutic response?

To answer this question, we investigate:

  • Engineering human organ microsystems through advanced biofabrication and modular organ-on-chip technologies.
  • Decoding disease mechanisms by examining how mechanical cues, extracellular matrix remodeling, and mechanotransduction regulate pulmonary health and disease.
  • Capturing immune complexity by integrating immune cells and host-pathogen interactions into physiologically relevant tissue models.
  • Advancing translational medicine through drug discovery, repurposing, and pharmacokinetic/pharmacodynamic (PK/PD) studies in human-relevant systems.

Our goal is to build predictive and clinically relevant organ microsystems that improve our understanding of disease and accelerate the development of effective therapies.
